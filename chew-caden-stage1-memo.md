## Executive Summary

This memo frames a comprehensive accounting ratio analysis of Caterpillar Inc. (NYSE: CAT) for fiscal year 2024. Caterpillar is a global leader in heavy machinery and equipment manufacturing, operating across construction, mining, and energy industries. The objective is to evaluate CAT's financial health across six ratio categories — Performance, Profitability, Efficiency, Leverage, Liquidity, and Du Pont — using data sourced from the company's FY2024 10-K filing. Raw financial statements reveal what happened; ratio analysis reveals why it matters, enabling management to benchmark CAT against peers and identify areas of strength or concern. This project will proceed through four stages, culminating in an Excel model, a technical specification, and an executive interpretation memo.

## Company Background & Objectives

**Company:** Caterpillar Inc. (NYSE: CAT)
**Industry:** Industrials — Heavy Machinery & Equipment
**Description:** Caterpillar designs, manufactures, and sells construction and mining equipment, diesel and natural gas engines, and industrial gas turbines to customers worldwide.

For FY2024 (ending December 31, 2024), CAT reported net sales of $64.8 billion and net income of approximately $11.6 billion. With total assets exceeding $100 billion and long-term debt of $25 billion, the company's financial position warrants a structured, multi-dimensional review. The CFO has requested this analysis to support strategic planning, capital allocation decisions, and investor communications.

Ratio analysis is the appropriate tool because raw financial statements, while accurate, do not provide intuitive comparisons across time or against industry peers. Ratios normalize the data, making it possible to assess whether CAT is becoming more efficient, whether its leverage is prudent, and whether its liquidity position is adequate to weather economic downturns in cyclical markets like construction and mining.

## Approach

This analysis will compute ratios across six standard categories using FY2024 income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow data sourced from Caterpillar's SEC EDGAR 10-K filing:

1. **Performance** — Measures how effectively CAT generates returns relative to its asset base and capital employed.
2. **Profitability** — Assesses margins at multiple levels (gross, operating, net) to evaluate cost control and pricing power.
3. **Efficiency** — Tracks how well CAT manages its assets, including inventory turnover, receivables, and asset utilization.
4. **Leverage** — Evaluates CAT's use of debt financing and the sustainability of its capital structure.
5. **Liquidity** — Determines CAT's ability to meet short-term obligations using current and quick ratios.
6. **Du Pont** — Decomposes return on equity into its component drivers to identify which factors most influence shareholder value.

Financial data inputs will be drawn from CAT's FY2024 and FY2023 10-K filings (SEC EDGAR), providing both current-year ratios and year-over-year comparisons.

## Limitations & Next Steps

**Known Limitations:** This analysis relies on a single company's reported financials without direct peer benchmarking in Stage 1. Ratios based on average balances (e.g., asset turnover, inventory days) require both FY2023 and FY2024 balance sheet data, which are included. The WACC estimate of 7.9% and effective tax rate of 21.4% are analyst assumptions that may be refined as the model develops.

**Next Steps:**

- **Stage 2:** Build a working Excel model that computes all ratios from real CAT financial data using named-range formulas, producing a clean and auditable ratio dashboard.
- **Stage 3:** Write a technical specification documenting the model's structure, named ranges, formulas used, and any assumptions or lessons learned during the build.
- **Stage 4:** Deliver a final executive memo with strategic interpretation of the ratios, actionable recommendations for management, and a structured AI prompt documenting how AI tools supported the analysis.
